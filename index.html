Mission

To eliminate preventable deaths from trauma and hemorrhagic shock by advancing innovative research, developing field-ready treatments, and rapidly translating military medical breakthroughs into lifesaving solutions for warfighters, first responders, and trauma patients worldwide.

Vision

A world where no soldier or civilian dies from survivable shock—where science, technology, and collaboration deliver rapid, effective, and deployable solutions in any environment: from point of injury, to evacuation, to definitive care.

Core values: Service to Those Who Serve • Scientific Excellence • Operational Realism • Collaboration • Integrity & Transparency • Innovation for Impact • Global Relevance

The Problem

Survivable shock still kills too many people.

Hemorrhagic shock remains a leading cause of preventable death after trauma—for warfighters on the battlefield and civilians in crashes, disasters, and violent injury. We know more about shock than ever before, yet gaps between research, field medicine, and systems of care leave lives on the table.

Situation Report
  • • Time-critical decisions with limited data at point of injury.
  • • Complex physiology under extreme stress and blood loss.
  • • Fragmented data across military, EMS, and hospital systems.
  • • Slow translation of breakthroughs into protocols and devices.

On the Battlefield

Combat medics and corpsmen operate with limited diagnostics, austere conditions, and tactical constraints. Every minute of uncontrolled bleeding reduces survival.

In Civilian Emergencies

EMS teams face unpredictable scenes, long transports, and variable resources. Coordination between EMS, trauma centers, and ICUs determines outcomes.

In Research & Development

Academia, startups, and traditional programs excel at discovery—but can struggle to sustain the focused, integrated, multi-year push needed to change standards of care across entire systems.

How Operation Lifeblood Works

A Focused Research Organization for Hemorrhagic Shock.

Operation Lifeblood is structured as a non-profit Functional Research Organization (FRO): a time-bound, milestone-driven effort with a single overriding mission—curing hemorrhagic shock as a survivable condition across military and civilian care.

FRO Principles
  • • Clear, ambitious, and finite mission.
  • • Dedicated full-time technical and clinical team.
  • • Milestones, not publications, as primary yardstick.
  • • Outputs as public goods: data, tools, protocols.
01

Time-Bound Mission

A 5–7 year horizon focused on moving hemorrhagic shock from unsolved challenge to operationally deployable solutions.

02

Integrated Team

Cardiac surgeons, trauma physicians, medics, data scientists, and engineers working as one mission-driven unit.

03

Milestone-Driven

Progress measured in validated models, protocols, devices, and clinical adoption—not just papers.

04

Public Good Focus

Data, tools, and guidelines designed for global use by militaries, EMS systems, and trauma centers.

Core Programs

Five lines of effort, one mission.

Cardiac Digital Twin for Hemorrhagic Shock

High-fidelity, patient-specific models of the heart and circulation to understand, predict, and guide treatment of hemorrhagic shock under battlefield and emergency conditions.

From physiologic simulation to decision support for medics, EMS, and trauma teams.

Translational Medicine for First Responders & Civilian Trauma Care

Adapting military shock protocols, devices, and diagnostics to EMS, emergency departments, and trauma centers worldwide.

Co-developed with frontline practitioners to fit real-world constraints and workflows.

Trauma Innovation Challenge Grants

Competitive grants to accelerate early-stage ideas in shock reversal, resuscitation strategies, novel therapeutics, and field diagnostics.

Designed to de-risk bold concepts and integrate them into the Lifeblood mission where they show promise.

Hemorrhagic Shock Data & Collaboration Network

A secure data commons connecting military, EMS, trauma centers, and research labs focused on hemorrhagic shock.

Enabling shared models, shared evidence, and shared progress while respecting privacy and security.

Advocacy & Policy Engagement

Elevating hemorrhagic shock as a sustained funding and policy priority across defense, public health, and emergency-care systems.

Engaging Congress, veterans’ groups, hospital systems, and international partners to align resources with the scale of the problem.

Who We Serve

Better tools, protocols, and support for those delivering lifesaving care under fire.

EMS, Fire & Rescue, Disaster Response

Practical advances that fit the realities of pre-hospital care and large-scale incidents.

Trauma Centers & Critical Care Teams

Integrated approaches from point of injury through ICU and recovery.

Veterans & Military Families

Reducing long-term impacts of combat injuries and improving survivorship and quality of life.

Global Health & Humanitarian Response

Making shock care advances accessible in low-resource and crisis-affected settings.
